## Design Context

### Users

Two primary audiences: **recruiters at tech companies** (scanning quickly, comparing candidates, need to see credibility and range fast) and **potential freelance clients** (need to trust the person, understand what they do, and feel like this person "gets" design). Both arrive with low patience — the portfolio must earn attention in seconds.

### Brand Personality

Creative, technical, good communicator. The interface should feel like a person who bridges two worlds — someone equally at home in a design tool and a terminal. Not a showoff, not invisible. Confident and precise.

**Emotional goal**: Visitors should feel they're dealing with someone who sweats the details without making it feel effortful. Trust + subtle excitement.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **Established palette**: Coral `#f55033` on near-black `#0c0c0b`, cream `#f0ede8` — raw, editorial, industrial
- **Typography**: Bebas Neue (display) + DM Mono (body) — intentional editorial pairing
- **Visual language**: Grid with thin border dividers, section numbering, `//` markers, crosshair cursor, monospace precision
- **Anti-references**: Generic AI-generated dark mode with purple-to-blue gradients, neon glow accents, glassmorphism, pastel palettes
- **Theme**: Dark only — the current direction is right, don't soften it

### Design Principles

1. **Precision over decoration** — every element earns its place; nothing is there to look cool
2. **Editorial rhythm** — the portfolio reads like a well-designed publication, not a template
3. **Craft is visible** — interactions, typography, and spacing signal attention to detail
4. **Honesty of voice** — copy and tone feel like a real person, not a LinkedIn summary
5. **Bridge both worlds** — the design must signal fluency in both engineering and design thinking
